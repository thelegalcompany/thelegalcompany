LEGAL AGREEMENTS

Legal Notice
The document that is sent to initiate legal proceedings against a certain person is known as a legal notice. Serving a legal notice is the first step for all legal litigations and matters. It can be drafted in plain paper and sent to start the legal proceedings.
Power Of Attorney
A power of attorney (POA) or letter of attorney is a written authorisation to represent or act on another’s behalf in private affairs, business, or some other legal matter, sometimes against the wishes of the other. The person who authorises the other to act is the principal, grantor, or donor (of the power).You can give someone the ability to do the following with POA:
  • -Write Cheques
  • -Sign official documents
  • -Handle other legal matters on your behalf
Right To Information
As per the Right to Information Act 2005, when a citizen requests for government information, timely response has to be given. Any citizen may request information from a “public authority” (a body of Government or “instrumentality of State”) under the provisions of the Act. The government is required to reply expeditiously or within thirty days.
Service Level Agreement
Service level agreement is a legal contract between the service provider and the service receiver. According to it, the delivery of the service has to be within the price and quality as has been pre-decided by both the parties. There are mainly two types of service level agreements – Business to Consumer (B2C) and Business to Business (B2B).
Termination Of Agreements
A termination of agreement is used by buyers and sellers of goods or services to terminate business contracts. When any of the parties of contract is dissatisfied with agreement they have made, either on the basis of fulfilment or on quality, they send this letter to terminate the agreement. Such termination can for any agreement and not just business.

Banking & Finance

Account Opening Resolution
An account opening resolution is a statement written to a bank when an account needs to be opened by the board of directors, in the name of the business/company. A board resolution passed during the Board Meeting authorises certain people of the company to open and operate the bank account.
Cheque Stop Payment Letter
This is a formal letter written to the cheque issuer’s bank requesting to stop the payment of the cheque issued. This request may be accepted only for transactions that are yet to be processed. A stop payment order is issued by the account holder, and can only be enacted if the cheque or payment has not already been processed by the recipient.
Promisory Note
This is a document that contains a written promise to pay a stated sum to a specified person at a specified date or on demand which has to be duly signed. Normally points like the principle amount, the interest rate (if any), the parties, date, terms of repayment, and maturity date is given. Other additional points may also be added.

EMPLOYMENT

Appointment Letter
When a company wants to officially appoint a person into the organisation, an appointment letter is a letter given. This is given after the person has accepted the job offer and now is being appointed as an employee. Normally such a letter is given on the first day of joining. It contains information about the position offered, like salary, benefits, company policies, and so on. The copy of the letter has to be signed by the employee and to the employer.
Employee Relieving Letter
An Employee Relieving letter is given by an employer to an employee after all the process of resignation is complete. So, the letter is give only once the resignation is submitted and accepted, and the notice period is served.
Employee Termination Notice
An Employment termination notice is given to an employee by an employer in a situation where, an employer due to one or more reasons, wants to terminate the employee from employment. Mainly it should contain several points such as reasons for termination, employment end date, notice period, and so on. The notice is to be given after proper reasoning and attempt to counsel the employee.
Employment Contract
An employment contract elucidates the terms and conditions of the employment and is an agreement between the employer and his employee.
Adding A Director Resolution
To Appoint a director a resolution has to be passed in the board meetings of the companies. Such resolution can be passed by a simple majority by the directors of the company subject to the maximum number of directors which can be appointed. On the passing of such resolution, the additional director can immediately use rights as a director of the company.
Offer Letter
An offer letter is a document that is given to a person by a company which has a formal job offer. This is given once the selection procedure is complete.
Resignation Letter
When an employee wishes to resign from his current position from a company, a resignation letter is given. The letter will contain details like request of date to be relieved, the reason for resignation, and duration of notice period. One must note that a resignation letter is valid only after it has been accepted by the employer.

BUSINESS AGREEMENTS

Extension Of Agreement
If you wish to continue an existing contract that is about to end, then, a Contract Extension Agreement is used. Instead of drafting a new agreement, changes in the termination date can be made to the old one. It is much simpler to use such an agreement instead of drafting a new one altogether. This agreement is also known as Extension of Contract Agreement and Contract Extension Letter.
Independent Contractor Agreement
An Independent Contractor Agreement is drawn to protect the interest and to govern the relationship between a company and an independent contractor.
Memorandum Of Understanding
A formal agreement between two or more parties that expresses convergence of will and indicates a common line of action between the two parties is known as a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU).
NDA For Third Party
A non- disclosure agreement or NDA is a contract that contains material, information, or knowledge that may be confidential and privy only to the parties of contract, while it is restricted to a third-party.
