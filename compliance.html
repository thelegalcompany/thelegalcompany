CORPORATE FILINGS
Annual Compliance for Private Company
A Private Company is an entity where the holdings stay within a closed group and needs to maintain its active status through the regular filing with MCA. It is compulsory to file audited financial statements and an annual return with the MCA for every company in each Financial Year. Irrespective of the turnover, it is mandatory to file the necessary documents with the RoC. It is also mandatory for every registered private limited company to conform to the annual compliances.
Both of these forms are filed to report the financial status and activities for the corresponding Financial Year. Based on the date of the Annual General Meeting, the due dates for annual filing is decided. Continuous failure to file may lead to the removal of the company’s name from RoC’s register and will also include directors’ disqualification. The MCA has actively taken bold steps to deal with any such events.
Annual Compliances for LLP
A Limited Liability Partnership enjoys a separate status, and so needs to maintain the active status by filing regularly with the MCA. For any LLP, annual compliance filing is mandatory, the business is operative or not. The annual compliance filing requires two separate forms - one for Annual Return, and the other for Statement of Accounts and Solvency.
The forms are filed for reporting the activities and financial data for each financial year in the upcoming year. The failure to fulfil LLP Annual Compliance requirements levies an additional fee of ₹ 100 each day of a delay till the actual date of filing. Hence, apart from the mandate, the heavy penalty compels the Designated Partners to fulfil the requirements.
DIR-3 KYC
MCA has aanounced mandatory filing of DIR-3 KYC for all DIN holders w.e.f 10th July 2018. It is now part of Rule 12A, Companies (Appointment and Qualifications of Directors) Rules, 2014. It is applicable to every DIN holder with ‘Approved’ status and also those who are disqualified.
Since this is an annual requirement, every DIN holder has to file the form within 30th April of immediate next financial year. The last date (i.e. 15th September, 2018) for filing the form for a person holding DIN on or before 31st March, 2018 has also been announced, so failure to do so will result in deactivation of DIN. To reactivate, the filing of the KYC has to be done along with payment of INR 5,000 as fees.
However, considering the technical difficulties faced by many to comply with the requirements of KYC, the ministry has allowed the filing at a reduced fee of INR 500 for 15 days starting from 21st September. From October 6th, fee of Rs 5,000 will be applicable for reactivation.
INC -20A
A company, incorporated on or after 02/11/2018, has to mandatorily file INC-20A with the MCA. It is also known by the name Declaration of Commencement of Business. It is the Directors who should file the same within 180 days from date of incorporation of a company which has share capital. Once the certificate of incorporation is given to the company, the directors has to file INC-20A. It is also mandatory to open a current account in a bank, to deposit the share capital which is subscribed in the MOA, by each and every promoter.
INC-22A
A new form INC-22A – ACTIVE (Active Company Tagging Identities and Verification) has been introduced by the MCA with effect from 25th February 2019. It has been inserted as Rule 25 A to the Companies (Incorporation) Rules, 2014. Every company incorporated on or before 31st December 2017 and which enjoy an ‘Active’ status, has to file this form on or before 25th April 2019. If the due date is not met, then the MCA will charge late filing fees of INR 10,000. Further, the company will be marked as ‘ACTIVE NON-COMPLAINT COMPANY’ and every DIN associated with such a company will be marked as ‘Director of ACTIVE non-compliant company’.
Registered Office Change
The registered office of a company is the address to which all official communications that pertains to the company are sent. Apart from a registered office, a company can also have a corporate office, an administrative office or branch office, factories, and so on. However, only the registered office address needs to be registered with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs. Opening of any other offices or additional locations does not require any intimation to the ROC.
It is the registered office of the Company that determines the domicile, that is, State of Incorporation. The state or location of the registered office of the Company will determine to which Registrar of Company the application for company registration must be made. In case of any change in the Registered Office address notification must be sent to the Registrar of Company (ROC) within 15 days.
Add Directors
Appointment of a director requires a resolution to be passed at a board meeting of the company. Such resolution can be passed by a simple majority by the directors of the company subject to the maximum number of directors which can be appointed. Once the resolution is passed, the additional director can use his rights as a director of the company immediately.
Remove Directors
Once the director submits his resignation letter to the board of director, then a resolution has to be passed in the board meeting of the company to make such resignation valid. After the resolution is passed, the said director would lose all his rights as director of the company immediately.
Increase Authorized Capital
Before issuing new equity shares and increasing the paid-up capital, a company may need to increase its authorised share capital. While paid-up capital is the total value of shares the company has issued, authorised share capital is the total value of shares a company can issue. Paid-up capital can never exceed authorised capital.
Share Transfer
Transfer of shares means the voluntary handing over of the rights and possibly, the duties as a member (as represented in a share of the company) from a shareholder who wishes to not be a member in the company any more to a person who wishes of becoming a member. Like any other movable property, the shares in a company are transferable in the absence of any expressed restrictions under the articles of the company.
MOA Amendment
A company is allowed to change its objects listed in its MOA, in accordance with the provisions of Section 13 of the Act. Accordingly, any alteration of MOA that pertains to the objects of the company is permitted through Special Resolution. Section 13 (8), however, restricts the change in object of a company that has raised money from public through prospectus and still has any unutilised amount from the money so raised. Such an act requires a special resolution to be passed by the company. The details of such resolution shall be published in one English language and one vernacular language newspaper in circulation at the place of registered office of the company as well as on the website of the company indicating the justification for such change in the object.
Winding Up Of Company
The process of dissolving a company is called winding up. While undergoing the winding up process, a company ceases to do the usual business. The sole purpose of the company then would be to sell off stock, pay off creditors, and distribute any remaining assets to shareholders or partners. Winding up is a legal process regulated by corporate laws, company’s articles of association/partnership agreement. Winding up can be compulsory or voluntary and is applicable to publicly and privately held companies.
Winding Up Of LLP
The Limited Liability Partnership is a hybrid form of entity with features of both the company and partnership. The Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008 and rules made there under has been framed for functioning and governing LLPs. In order to reduce the number of bogus entities, it is important to provide an ease in procedure of winding up. The two ways of winding up of LLP is voluntary winding up and compulsory winding up (by tribunal).
CORPORATE
No Objection Certificate (For Address Proof)
If someone wishes to register a property that isn’t self-owned as their official business address, then a ‘No Objection Certificate’ is required. The owner of the property has to provide an NOC which should also mention that he is aware of the current business on his property and has no objections regarding it.
Pvt Ltd Company To LLP Resolution
When a Private Limited Company is converted to a Limited Liability Partnership, this resolution has to be passed in the board meeting. The resolution takes immediate effect once it is duly signed by the authorized director and the private limited company gets converted into Limited Liability Partnership.
Private Limited Company To Public Limited Company Resolution
When a Private Limited Company is to be converted to a Public limited company, this resolution has to be passed in the board meeting. The resolution takes immediate effect once it is duly signed by the authorized director and the Private Limited Company gets converted into a Public limited Company.
Resignation Of Director Resolution
Once the director submits his resignation letter addressed to the board of director, a resolution has to be passed in the board meeting of the company to make the resignation valid. After the resolution is passed the director would immediately lose his rights as director in the company.
Shareholders Agreement
This agreement is drawn in between the shareholders of a company where they decide, the management, structure, directors, shareholding and control of the company, their rights, and obligations. A shareholders agreement regulate shareholders rights, management, and operation policy of the company.
PROJECT REPORT
Project Report is a written document relating to any investment that contains data, on the basis of which the project has been appraised and found feasible. The information covers the economic, technical, managerial, financial, and production aspects. With this information, the entrepreneur gets to know the inputs and also helps him obtain loans from banks or financial Institutions.

The report has detailed information regarding land and building the company requires, manufacturing capacity per annum, machinery & equipment, along with their prices and specifications, manufacturing process, the raw materials required, power & water requirement, manpower needs, marketing cost of the project, production, financial analysis, and economic viability of the project.

A projected profit and loss account and balance sheet must be presented in the project report to judge the profitability of the business. It must show the estimated sales revenue, cost of production, gross profit and net profit likely to be earned by the proposed unit. In addition to the above, a projected balance sheet, cash and funds flow statement must be prepared every year for a period of at least 3 to 5 years.

The income statement and cash flow projections should include a three-year summary, detail by month for the first year, and detail by quarter for the second and third years. Break-even point and rate of return on investment must be stated along with the accounting system and the inventory control system. The project report must also state whether the business is economically and financially viable.

The proposed units draw inputs from the society and so its contribution to the society in the form of employment, income, exports, and infrastructure must also be shown. The output of the business must be indicated in the project report.
