What is a Private Limited Company ?

A Private Limited Company is a registered corporate structure, incorporated under the Companies Act of 2013, and governed by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA). It has a separate legal identity from its owners which provides advantages like the ability to enter into contract in its own name and safeguard personal assets of the owners from business liabilities.

ADVANTAGES & BENEFITS

Limited Liability Protection to Directors personal assets

A startup would at many times, need to take things on credit and borrow money. In case of a Partnership, the personal property and savings would be at risk if the business is unable to repay its loans. In a private limited company, however, the personal assets of the directors are safe because only the investment in business is lost.

Better image and credibility in Market

Private limited company is a well-known business structure which is also quite popular. Corporate Customers, Vendors and Govt. Agencies prefer dealing with Private Limited Company instead of normal partnerships or proprietorship.

Easy to raise funds and loans

A Private Limited Company has a wide range of options when it comes to raising funds like Bank Loans, Venture Capitalists, Angel Investors, in comparison to LLPs and OPCs. Vendors and Govt. Agencies prefer dealing with Private Limited Company instead of normal partnerships or proprietorship.

Favorite Business structure for Investors

Private Limited companies are well structured and has lesser complications in procedures, so investors prefer investing here. Most important it is easy to exit from a Private Limited Company.

Easy to attract Employees

Putting together a team and ensuring their stay for a longer period is a challenge for startups. Since there is a certain confidence attached to a Private Limited structure, it is easier to hire people as well as motivate them with corporate designations and stock options.

Easy to Sell

A Private Limited Company can be sold easily with minimal documentation and cost.

One of the Directors must be Indian Resident
DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) for 2 Promoters & 1 witness
Minimum Authorised Share Capital 100,000 (INR One Lac)
The directors and shareholders can be same person
DIN (Director Identification Number) for all Directors
